Submitting and Rating Dishes

How do I submit a new dish?

1. Once you are logged in, click the “Submit a dish” button in the top right.

   It expand to display fields to fill in.

2. Enter the restaurant name which should hopefully auto-fill

 - If it does not find the restaurant click the “Add a new restaurant” link below

-  Search for the restaurant and once found click “Add to Topdish” button

4. Enter the dish name  (if the dish already exists it should pre-populate it)

5. Select the dish category and price

6. Enter the description as you would see it on the menu

7. Enter your thoughts about the dish. (Use the TopDish glossary of descriptors to assist you.)

8. Submit a picture by clicking the photo icon

9. Rate the dish - Would you recommend it?

10. Click the “Submit” button to confirm your entry

What area do each of the meal categories cover

Breakfast: Breakfast and Brunch - anything more common to have in the morning or is classified on the breakfast/brunch menu.

Side: A menu item that says its a side or is complementary dish to your main entree

Sandwich: If it has Sandwich in the title or is categorized under Sandwiches in the menu. If it is anything  between two pieces of bread, it most likely falls under the Sandwich category

Soup: If it is categorized as soup on the menu. A majority of the dish is liquid based.

Salad: If it is categorized as a Salad on the menu

Starter: Appetizer/Small Plate  - any dish that is not a meal by itself. Usually it would be shared with several people and comes before another larger portion dish

Entree: Main Plate - The main course of the meal

Dessert: Categorized as dessert on the menu. Usually sweet and had at the end of a meal

Drink: If it is categorized as drink, beverages, cocktails, beer and or wine on a menu

What if a dish has multiple dish categories?

Select the primary dish category for the dish. We know some may fall into multiple and plan on adding different menus. For now select the most relevant choice. For instance an omelet might be available for lunch but it is more applicable to be put under the breakfast/brunch category.

We are working on a way to display this information

What type of information should I include in the menu description?

Use the existing menu description as a start. Try and be detailed as possible including the ingredients. But feel free to add your own touch. The best example is to think of how a waiter and a nice restaurant would describe a special of the day. Get people to salivate when they hear the dish description.

What if I don’t know the menu description?

If you do not know the ingredients or do not have a menu description, just type N/A

What should I include in my review?

Keep it short and sweet, we are not looking for page long essays. The best reviews are the ones that can help someone else make a better decision. Tell us what you specifically liked or did not like about the dish. We know not everyone is food critic so use our glossary as an aid to figure out the right words to say. If you want to go the extra mile search google and see if someone has a better description of the dish.

How do I rate an existing Dish?

1. Click the up or down arrow next to a dish which will open a new window to review and submit a picture.

What do the numbers above and below the arrows represent?

1. The blue number above the up arrow represents the number of positive votes the dish has received. These are users who would recommend you try the dish.

2. The orange number below the down arrow represents the number of negative reviews. These are users who would not recommend it.

Flagging content

On a dish level page there is a flag icon for each review which allows you to classify it as one of the following

  1. Its spam! (advertising, unrelated links, etc.)
  2. Its inappropriate! (vulgar, adult, hurtful, etc.)
  3. Its inaccurate! (misspellings, duplicate, misplaced, etc.)
